health_and_safety Health & Basics
Your ductwork is the respiratory system of your home. Over time it accumulates dust, pollen, dander, mold spores, and debris that get recirculated every time your HVAC runs. Regular cleaning maintains indoor air quality (IAQ) and directly impacts the health of everyone in the house — especially children, the elderly, and allergy sufferers.
We typically recommend a thorough cleaning every 3–5 years for most households. However, if you have pets, recent renovations, known mold issues, or anyone with allergies or asthma in the home, we recommend every 2–3 years. A quick call with our team can help you determine the right schedule.
Watch for excessive dust accumulation on surfaces shortly after cleaning, musty or stale odors when your system runs, visible dust or debris around vents and registers, increased allergy or respiratory symptoms indoors, or if it's been more than 5 years since your last service.
Yes, significantly. Ductwork traps pollen, pet dander, dust mites, and other allergens that get blown back into living spaces every time your system cycles. Removing that reservoir of irritants directly reduces the allergen load in your home's air and is one of the most effective steps you can take for allergy relief.
military_tech Process & Logistics
A typical residential duct cleaning takes 2–4 hours depending on the size of your home and the condition of your ductwork. Dryer vent cleaning usually takes 45–60 minutes. We'll give you a time estimate when you schedule.
We start with a detailed inspection and, when possible, camera verification of problem areas. We then use a high-powered HEPA vacuum to create negative pressure in the duct system, pulling contaminants out rather than pushing them through your home. All registers, returns, and air handlers are cleaned. We finish with a post-cleaning inspection and provide before/after documentation.
Consumer-grade vacuums and brushes can't reach deep into the ductwork and may actually dislodge contaminants without fully removing them, making air quality temporarily worse. Our commercial-grade HEPA equipment creates negative pressure across the entire system, safely extracting debris. DIY is not recommended and can void HVAC warranties or damage flexible ductwork.
Yes. We offer comprehensive air quality testing, airflow efficiency testing, and HVAC performance testing. These services can be performed standalone or combined with a cleaning. Testing gives you quantified data on your home's air quality before and after service.
